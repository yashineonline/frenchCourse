# Exam Strategy & Mocks (TEF/TCF)

This page helps you turn study into **test score**: what to do, how to time it, and how to self-correct.

---

## Choose your test: TEF Canada or TCF Canada
Both are accepted by IRCC for Express Entry (verify current rules on IRCC).  
Pick the one with:
- earlier test dates near you
- a format you prefer
- more prep materials available to you

---

## Your target (common PR-optimized goal)
Aim for **NCLC 7** in **Listening, Reading, Writing, Speaking**.

---

## The 80/20 scoring rules (works for both TEF/TCF)
### 1) Clarity beats complexity
Short sentences, correct tense, clear structure.

### 2) Structure is “free points”
Always follow:
- answer
- 2 reasons
- 1 example
- conclusion

### 3) Use connectors (but don’t overdo)
Minimum 3 per task:
- d’abord, ensuite, enfin
- cependant / en revanche
- donc / par conséquent
- par exemple

### 4) Repeat key nouns (avoid pronoun confusion)
Instead of “ça / ceci / cela” too often, repeat the main word:
- *le permis*, *le logement*, *le rendez-vous*, etc.

## Speaking strategy (fast)
### Interaction tasks (calls / roleplay)
Your goal is to **ask good questions** and confirm details.

Use this loop:
1) Situation + intention  
2) Ask 3–5 questions  
3) Confirm (“Donc si je comprends bien…”)  
4) Close politely (“Merci, bonne journée.”)

**Question bank (high-frequency)**
- C’est combien ?
- Quels documents faut-il ?
- Quels sont les délais ?
- Est-ce que c’est inclus ?
- Est-ce qu’il y a des conditions ?
- Pouvez-vous répéter / épeler ?
- Quelle est la prochaine étape ?

### Opinion tasks
Use the 60-second skeleton:
- Position (1 sentence)
- Reason 1
- Reason 2
- Example
- Conclusion + nuance

---

## Writing strategy (fast)
### Email/message tasks
Template:
- Objet
- Bonjour Madame/Monsieur
- Je vous écris concernant…
- Je souhaiterais…
- Serait-il possible de… ?
- Merci / Cordialement

### Opinion tasks (longer)
Template:
- Intro: position
- 2–3 paragraphs: arguments + 1 example
- Counterpoint: “Cependant…”
- Conclusion: synthèse + ouverture

---

## Self-correction (10 minutes only)
Don’t “correct everything”. Choose ONE focus:
- verbs (tense)
- agreements (gender/plural)
- connectors
- pronunciation (one sound)

---

## Weekly mock routine (recommended)
### Week A (lighter)
- 1 speaking roleplay (record, 6–8 min total)
- 1 writing task (timed)

### Week B (heavier)
- 2 speaking tasks
- 2 writing tasks
- 1 listening session with summary

---

## Mock templates you can reuse
### Mini mock (30 minutes)
1) Speaking: 1 roleplay prompt (8 min)
2) Writing: 1 email (15 min)
3) Review: pick 5 errors to fix (7 min)

### Full mock (90 minutes)
1) Speaking: 2 prompts (record)
2) Writing: 1 short + 1 long
3) Review: rewrite intro/conclusion for the long writing

---

## Score yourself (simple rubric)
Rate each 1–5:
- Clarté (understandable)
- Organisation (structure)
- Vocabulaire (precise words)
- Grammaire (verbs + agreement)
- Prononciation (for speaking) / Orthographe (for writing)
